## The author’s thesis

On the timeline, the suspect likely ran **Iran-focused human intelligence**—paying foreign agents—while allegedly **pocketing** funds meant for sources and keeping bullion at home.

Reported figures cited in the answer:

- **Nov 2025–Mar 2026**: withdrew large amounts of foreign currency and **303 bars** of about **1 kg** each, justified as **official / covert-action expenses**  
- At roughly **1,000 CNY/gram**, the metal alone is on the order of **~303 million CNY**; the author also mentions a **~$40M** scale of related funds  

Covert budgets are often **not reimbursable like normal travel**; paying assets in cash or gold is plausible in theory—**storing bars at home** is not.

## Why Iran-focused intel?

The author’s reasoning (speculative):

1. Peacetime tradecraft rarely sees **this density** of gold withdrawals; low-value reporting would not earn “bars per tip.”  
2. U.S. focus in that window included Venezuela and Iran; Venezuela-related action largely ended by **January**, yet withdrawals continued through **March**—suggesting **Iran**.  
3. Only Iran, in that period, justified such **secret spending** in the author’s view.  
4. **War with Iran** broke out around **late Feb 2026**, while the gold spike was **pre-war**, under “covert need” paperwork.

The author further speculates: fabricated sources and reports ahead of war; U.S. forces may have acted on bad assessments (e.g. expecting rapid regime collapse), were unprepared for drones/missiles, and when operations stalled, intel became the scapegoat—**analysis only, not a court finding**.

## Audit, FBI, and the “tradecraft hole”

Press accounts describe a **routine CIA audit** finding assets not on the books, an internal probe, and referral to the **FBI** under DCI **John Ratcliffe**.

The answer notes the paradox of covert accounting: funds can vanish into a **black hole**, and “the gold bought intel” is a standard defense—**but not if it sits in your house**. A “smarter” cover story (per the author) would hide disposal and claim an active Iran network still maturing.

## Takeaway

The post treats media coverage as incomplete: the case highlights **unauditable covert money**, scale, and overlap with the **Iran war timeline**. Full argument and discussion are on the Zhihu source link.
