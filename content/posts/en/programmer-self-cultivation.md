> This post is adapted from a Zhihu article (Chinese): [没想到一书《程序员的自我修养》对我启发如此大？？？](https://www.zhihu.com/tardis/zm/art/187305388), by @一包伤心的辣条. It summarizes *The Self-Cultivation of Programmers* by Chen Yihe. Rights belong to the original author and publisher.

The full article is available in Chinese at `/zh/blog/programmer-self-cultivation`. Below is a brief English overview of the main themes.

## Career

- Know yourself; outwork average effort when other advice fails.
- Build authority through real skill, then show it at the right time.
- Follow best practices; stay curious; communicate beyond code.
- Work with strong people; treat life, sleep, and travel as part of growth.
- Pay is tied to how hard you are to replace, not raw workload.
- Deep involvement in one project beats shallow touches on many.
- Keep learning; protect your reputation with rigor.
- Don't say "easy" or "impossible" without analysis; collaborate instead of solo heroics.
- Learn self-marketing: read outside tech, write clear docs.

## Practice and thinking

- See the problem behind the technology (e.g. missing user feedback vs. "system broken").
- Speak up like an expert when you truly understand; learn broadly for perspective.
- Regain creativity through daily reading, weekly blogging, and capturing fleeting ideas.

## Personal development

- Never stop learning; admit mistakes quickly.
- Experts differ in **awareness**—not just experience.
- Prefer "wet" learning (reflection, talks, writing) over hoarding "dry" listicles you never apply.
