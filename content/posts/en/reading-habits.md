Many people set a goal of “how many books per year.” That’s fine, but fixating on the number can turn reading into a checklist and drain the joy out of it.

## Small steps, sustained

Fifteen to thirty minutes a day is often more sustainable than binge-reading an entire book on weekends. A few pages in spare moments add up.

## Notes and output

After reading, jot down what moved you, or try explaining it to someone else. Sharing what you learned is a good test of real understanding.

## Allow yourself to slow down

Some books deserve a close read—or a reread; others you can skim and move on. You don’t need to “finish” every title. Pick what helps you right now.

Reading is ultimately about widening your view and nourishing your thinking—not filling shelves or lists. Finding your own pace matters more than any method.
