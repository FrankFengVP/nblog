> Adapted from a Zhihu column: [Karpathy on the December inflection](https://zhuanlan.zhihu.com/p/2010321524024943014). Rights belong to the original author.

**Karpathy**, who coined Vibe Coding, draws a blunt timeline: before **last December**, coding agents were “basically useless”; after December, **everything flipped**.

## Not gradual—a phase change

This wasn’t slow progress. Models gained **long-horizon consistency** and could finish large, tedious jobs. The era of typing every line is ending; the new job is **orchestrating agents**—tools, memory, prompts, then reviewing their work. He calls high-end practice **agentic engineering**, with unusually high leverage right now.

## Strong engineers get multiplied

Karpathy pushes back on replacement fear: deep engineers aren’t obsolete; their leverage can **multiply** when they own architecture and judgment while agents supply execution volume.

## What shipped around December?

| When | What |
|------|------|
| 2025-11-19 | Google Gemini 3 |
| 2025-11-25 | Claude Opus 4.5 |
| 2025-12-12 | GPT-5.2 |
| 2026-02 | Opus 4.6; GPT-5.3-Codex response |

Release timing lines up with the usability jump.

## Workflow implications

1. **Typing → conducting**—you ship task specs and acceptance criteria  
2. **Chat → long jobs**—agents maintain goals across tool steps  
3. **Model → harness**—same weights, different agent shells, very different UX  

If your mental model is still pre-December, you may be a generation behind.
